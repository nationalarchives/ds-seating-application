The National Archives takes pride in the integrity and condition of our collection. Please read and accept our terms and conditions before ordering. - The reading rooms are designated quiet zones. You must respect the right of other readers to research in a quiet environment. - All equipment must be switched to silent mode (mobile phones, laptops, cameras etc). - All camera flash functions must be disabled. - You must not bend, fold, untag, overhang, separate, mark or cause damage to any document in any way. - If you wish to either photograph or have a copy made of a document you must take the whole document with you, not just the part that you wish to copy. - You must use the available preservation equipment to ensure that the documents are not damaged in any way. - If approached by a member of staff regarding a breach of any of the above points you must adhere to their instruction. If you are unsure of any procedures please seek advice from a member of staff. Failure to adhere to our rules may result in the removal of your right to use the records. A full set of rules can be found at: http://www.nationalarchives.gov.uk/documents/rules.pdf
